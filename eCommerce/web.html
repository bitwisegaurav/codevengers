Web Based E-Commerce

Web-based e-commerce refers to the buying and selling of goods and services over the internet. It involves conducting business transactions electronically, allowing customers to browse and purchase products or services online.

Advantages

  • Cost effective
  • Easily customizable
  • Build brand credibility
  • Cheap and environment friendly
  • Save time and money
  • Access your business information from anywhere at anytime
  • Improve the interactivity with customers
  • Easier installation and maintenance
  • Manage your suppliers
  • Get customers feedback on your business
  • Offer services online
  • Allow customers to make reservations or appointments online
  • Manage financecs
  • Research competitors

Steps in setting up buisness on internet

There are some steps to follow :

Discovering

The initial phase is to discover the market demands.

Following requirements are considered in this phase :

  • Business Product
  • Target Audience
  • Set short/long term objectives
  • Gathering buisness, functional, technical, infrastructure requirements
  • Database requirements
  • E commerce compatibility
  • Domain procurement

Defining

After discovering the requirements, the next step is to define the requirements. This involves discussing your project planning, requirements definations, marketing goals, budget, etc. Some of requirements in this phase :

  • Prepare project plan and schedule
  • Set deliverables milestones
  • Resources and funding plan
  • Demo site flowchart
  • SEO and ROI Analysis
  • Payment processors

Registering

Registering refers to the process of signing up for access to a system or service, typically by providing personal information and creating a user account.

Designing

It includes :

  • Prototyping
  • Functional design
  • Technical design
  • System Architecture
  • Training Plan
  • Test Plan

Developing

Developing refers to the process of creating the website or application based on the design and requirements defined in the previous steps.

During the development phase, the developer generally focuses on application coding, Testing assurance, Quality Assurance, User and program documentations.

  • Coding and conversion
  • Cascading Style Sheets
  • Content Management Design
  • SEO
  • Inventory Management System
  • Analytics Setup
  • Data integration
  • Social media integration

Deploying

It includes :

  • Website upload
  • Database Setup
  • Hardware/Software installation
  • Data Conversion
  • User Training
  • Support Training
  • Post Implementation Review
  • Analytics Reporting
  • SEO
  • Tutorials
  • Monthly maintenance

Promoting

After Deploying, next step is to get the traffic on website which can be done through SEO and SEM/PPC (Search Engine Marketing/Pay Per Click). Advertise on social media.

Strategies for business on internet

A good plan for any business over web should cover :

Planning and Strategy

  • Concept and Objective : Identifying the purpose and goals of the business.
  • Model : Creating a framework for the business operations.
  • Buisness Strategy : Developing a plan to achieve the business objectives.
  • Operational Strategy : Determining the approach for day-to-day operations.
  • Legal Research : Conducting research on legal requirements and regulations.

Market Research

  • Market Research : Gathering information about the target market and customers.
  • Design and Usability Research : Assessing the design and usability of the website.
  • Competitive Research : Analyzing the competition in the market.
  • Brand Strategy : Developing a strategy to establish and promote the brand.

Design and Development

  • Project Management : Planning, organizing, and managing the development process.
  • Technology Selection : Choosing the appropriate technologies for the website.
  • Content : Creating and organizing the content for the website.
  • SEO and SEM : Optimizing the website for search engines and running online marketing campaigns.
  • Wireframing : Creating visual representations of the website's layout and structure.
  • Design & Development : Implementing the visual design and coding the website.

Deployment and Maintenance

  • Beta testing, Bug fixing and Usability testing : Testing the website for issues and improving its usability.
  • Hosting and Security : Setting up the website on a server and ensuring its security.
  • Go live : Making the website accessible to the public.
  • Maintenance : Regularly updating and maintaining the website.
  • Version control and upgrades : Managing changes and improvements to the website.

Features of E commerce website

  • Product Catalog : Displaying products and services with images, descriptions, and prices.
  • Shopping Cart : Allowing customers to add products to a virtual shopping cart for purchase.
  • Payment Gateway : Processing online payments securely and efficiently.
  • Order Management : Managing orders, tracking shipments, and handling returns.
  • Customer Accounts : Allowing customers to create accounts, view order history, and save payment information.
  • Search Functionality : Enabling customers to search for products by category, keyword, or other criteria.
  • Responsive Design : Ensuring the website is optimized for mobile devices and different screen sizes.
  • SEO : Optimizing the website for search engines to improve visibility and attract more visitors.
  • Analytics : Tracking website traffic, user behavior, and sales data to make informed decisions.
  • Security : Implementing security measures to protect customer data and prevent fraud.
  • Social Media Integration : Integrating social media platforms to promote products and engage with customers.
  • Customer Support : Providing customer service through live chat, email, or phone.
  • Reviews and Ratings : Allowing customers to leave reviews and ratings for products and services.
  • Newsletter : Sending regular updates and promotions to customers through email.

Components of E Commerce website

  1. E commerce software/Shopping Cart : Software that allows customers to shop and checkout.
  2. A merchant account : A bank account that allows businesses to accept payments.
  3. Hosting : A service that makes your website accessible via the World Wide Web.
  4. Product Information : Details about the products being sold.
  5. Web Traffic : The amount of data sent and received by visitors to a website.
  6. Web Analytics Software : Tools that track and report website data.
  7. Security and Backup : Measures to protect the website and its data.
  8. Multiple payment options : Providing customers with various ways to pay.
  9. Personalization : Customizing the shopping experience based on user behavior.
  10. Appearance : The visual aspect of the website, including layout and graphics.
  11. Contents : The text, images, videos, and other media on the website.
  12. SEO : Techniques to improve a website's visibility on search engine results pages.
  13. Cross selling and up selling : Strategies to sell more products to customers.
  14. Navigational options : Features that help users navigate the website.
  15. Feedback and customer service : Channels for customers to provide feedback or get help.

Building phase in Website Development

In House

Building a website in-house involves hiring a team of developers, designers, and other professionals to create the website from scratch. This approach gives you full control over the design, functionality, and content of the website, but it can be time-consuming and expensive.

Outsourcing

Outsourcing involves hiring a third-party web development company or freelancer to build the website for you. This approach can be more cost-effective and faster than building in-house, but it may require you to give up some control over the project.

Elements of a webpage

  • Header or banner : The top section of a webpage that typically contains the logo and main navigation.
  • Navigation bar : A menu that allows users to navigate through different sections of a website.
  • Page content : The main body of a webpage that contains the information or media being presented.
  • Footer : The bottom section of a webpage that often includes additional links and copyright information.
  • Background : The visual backdrop of a webpage that sets the overall theme or atmosphere.
  • Sidebar : A vertical column on the side of a webpage that provides additional information or navigation options.

Web Authoring Tools

It is a piece of software which allows you to design and create pages.

Word Processors

Software used to create, edit, format, and print documents. Examples include Microsoft Word and Google Docs.

Desktop Publishing Programs

Software used to create layout-intensive documents like brochures, flyers, and magazines. Examples include Adobe InDesign and Microsoft Publisher.

HTML Editors

Software used to create and edit web pages using HTML and other web languages. Examples include Sublime Text and Atom.

Software used to edit plain text files. They are often used for coding, writing scripts, and more. Examples include Notepad++ and Vim.

Online tools that allow users to create websites using pre-built elements and templates. Examples include Wix and Squarespace.

Web Hosting

Web hosting is a service that allows individuals and organizations to post a website or web page onto the Internet. A web host, or web hosting service provider, is a business that provides the technologies and services needed for the website or webpage to be viewed on the Internet.

Types of Web Hosting

  • Shared Web Hosting : Multiple websites share the same server and resources, making it an affordable option for small websites.
  • Dedicated Hosting : A single website has an entire server to itself, providing more power and control but at a higher cost.
  • Reseller Hosting : Allows the account owner to use their allotted hard drive space and bandwidth to host websites on behalf of third parties.
  • Managed Hosting : The hosting provider takes care of server maintenance and management tasks, freeing up the website owner to focus on other aspects of their business.
  • Cloud Hosting : Websites are hosted on a network of connected virtual and physical cloud servers, offering scalability and reliability.
  • Cluster Hosting : Multiple servers host the same website to improve load balancing and failover, enhancing performance and availability.

Web Traffic Analysis

Web traffic analysis is the process of monitoring and analyzing the behavior of visitors to a website. It involves collecting data on how users interact with a website, such as which pages they visit, how long they stay on each page, and what actions they take while on the site.

Web Metrics

  • Visits : The number of times a user enters a site during a given period.
  • Number of visits : The total count of individual sessions initiated by all the users to your site.
  • Number of page views : The total number of pages viewed. Repeated views of a single page are counted.
  • Unique visitors : Individuals who have visited a site at least once during a reporting period.
  • Hits : The total number of requests made to the server during a given period.
  • Bounce Rates : The percentage of visitors who navigate away from the site after viewing only one page.
  • Avg. Time on page : The average amount of time a user spends viewing a specific page or screen.
  • Value per visit : The average value generated by each website visit, calculated by dividing the total online revenue by the number of total visits.
