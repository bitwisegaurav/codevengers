E-Payment Process

It involves a buyer and a seller. Buyer who makes a payment. A seller who receives a payment. And the process to transfer monetary value from the buyer to the seller is known as E-Payment.

An e payment process involves following steps :

  • The buyer and seller set yp accounts in financial institutions (or banks) that allow them to make or accept payments.
  • Buyer provide payment information (like bank account number or credit card numbers) from where payments should be withdraw and sellers give information about where payments should be deposited.
  • Buyer select products from seller's website and choose their preferred payment method.
  • Buyer enter payment information which goes to secure server as encrypted using security technologies.
  • The payment information moves to the online transaction server where the payment will be authorized or declined depending on the accuracy of information.
  • If information is valid then it is transmitted to the financial institution that receives payments owed to the buyer and a deposit is made to the sellers bank account.
  • Buyer and seller receive confirmation of the transaction.

Phases in E-Payment

There are four phases in e-payment process :

  1. Registration : It involves registration of buyer and seller with corresponding banks respectively in order to create link between their identities and their accounts held at bank.
  2. Invoicing : Buyer obtains an invoice (proof of purchase) for payment from the seller.
  3. Payment Selection and Processing : It involves the selection of payment type (card, e-cash, e-cheque, etc.). After that buyer sends its relevant pament details (like account number, unique identifiers) to seller along with accepted amount based on invoice.
  4. Payment Authorization and Confirmation : On receiving the payment details from the seller, the seller's bank authroizes the payment and issues a receipt containing the sucess or failure of the payment.

Advantages of E-Payment

  • Increased Speed and Convenience : It is faster and more convenient than traditional payment methods like cash or cheque.
  • Increased Sales : It allows businesses to reach a wider audience and increase their sales.
  • Time Saving : It saves time for both buyers and sellers by eliminating the need to handle cash or cheques.
  • Reduced Costs : It reduces the cost of handling cash or cheques and eliminates the need for physical infrastructure like bank branches.
  • Variety of Payment Options : It offers a variety of payment options like credit cards, debit cards, e-wallets, and mobile payments.
  • Highly Effective Security : It provides a high level of security through encryption and authentication technologies.
  • Improved Customer Support : It allows businesses to provide better customer support by offering real-time payment processing and tracking.
  • User-Friendly : It is easy to use and user-friendly, making it accessible to a wide range of users.

Types of E-Payment Systems

There are several types of e-payment systems available in the market. Some of them are :

  1. Electronic Cash (E-Cash) : It is a digital form of cash that can be used for online payments. It is a secure and convenient way to make payments online.
  2. Electronic Cheque (E-Cheque) : It is an electronic version of a paper cheque. It is used to make payments online and is a secure and convenient way to transfer money.
  3. Electronic Fund Transfer (EFT) : It is a system that allows the transfer of money from one bank account to another bank account electronically. It is a secure and convenient way to transfer money.
  4. Debit Card : It is a card that allows the holder to transfer money electronically from their bank account when making a purchase. It is a secure and convenient way to make payments.
  5. Credit Card : It is a card that allows the holder to borrow money from the bank to make purchases. It is a secure and convenient way to make payments.
  6. Mobile Payment : It is a payment made using a mobile phone. It is a secure and convenient way to make payments.
  7. Online Banking : It is a system that allows the transfer of money from one bank account to another bank account online. It is a secure and convenient way to transfer money.

Credit Cards

It is a small plastic card having a magnetic strip issued by a bank or a non-banking financial company (NBFC) which allows holder to purchase goods or servicecs on credit.

A credit card holder can purchase products or services without cash and to pay for them at a later date. The consumer must open an account with a bank or compnay. The card issuer gices a credit limit with a specified amount.

Every month the sponsor provides a bill, which tallies the card activity during previous 30 days. Depending on terms of card, customer may pay interest charges on the amount that they do not pay for on a monthly basis.

Debit Cards

It is a plastic electronic card issued by a bank which allows bank clients access to their account to withdraw cash or pay for goods or services.

Unlike credit cards, the debit card immediately transfers money from the client's account to the business account. Hence the debit card also acts as prepaid card.

With the debit card, the customer can only pay for purchased goodds with money that is already there in his/her bank account. So, it is important for card holder to keep sufficient amount of cash balance in its bank account.

The bank takes nominal charges as an annual fee for the issuance and maintenance of card.

E-Cash (Digital Cash)

It is a form of an E-Payment in which certain amount of money is stored on a client device (PC or mobile) and made accessible for internet transaction. It is also known as Cybercash, Digital cash or E-Money.

E-cash allows a customer to make an anonymous purchase over the internet.

Smart Cards

It is a plastic card with a microchip embedded in it which is used for identification or financial transactions. These cards have self contained operating systems and so are ideal for applications where security is an issue. Their memory capacity is also considerably larger than that of the more conventional magstripe cards. The normal life of chips in smart cards are of atleast five years.

A smart card is more secure than a magnetic stripe card. If you enter an incorrect password too many times, the card will be disabled temporarily.

The greatest disadvantage of using credit is that most of the information must be stored in online mainframe computer networks, from where it is verified and processed. Smart cards however, with an embedded microprocesssor store information since all the security required is embedded in microprocesssor.

Traditional Payment

Traditional payment methods like cash, cheque, and credit card are still widely used in many parts of the world. However, these methods have several disadvantages compared to e-payment systems. Some of the disadvantages of traditional payment methods are :

  • Lack of Convenience : Traditional payment methods like cash and cheque are not as convenient as e-payment systems. They require physical handling and are time-consuming.
  • Lack of Security : Traditional payment methods like cash and cheque are not as secure as e-payment systems. They are prone to theft and fraud.
  • Lack of Coverage : Traditional payment methods like cash and cheque are not as widely accepted as e-payment systems. They are limited to certain locations and businesses.
  • Lack of Eligibility : Traditional payment methods like cash and cheque are not as accessible as e-payment systems. They require a bank account or credit card to use.
  • Lack of Speed : Traditional payment methods like cash and cheque are not as fast as e-payment systems. They require physical handling and processing.

Online third party verified payment system

Third party services act like a warrantor who ensures the resolve of any online transaction disputes if occurs, satisfy both buyers and sellers.

Both buyer and seller registers on third party service. Buyer get their account number. It provides merchant services to merchant/seller. Merchant accounts authorize suppliers to accept credit or debit cards payments online.

Working

  • Buyer requests for a product online and authorizes the supplier to access the account number and bill.
  • Supplier contacts the third party payment processor with buyers account number.
  • Third party payment processor work in the background to verify the account number, checks for adequate funds availability and bill the buyer's account.
  • Third party payment servre sends an eletronic message to buyer and buyer responds by agreeing or not agreeing.
  • If server gets acceptance, the supplier is informed.
  • Once the process gets approval, the payment processors take the money from buyers bank account and deliver it to the suppliers bank account.

Payment Gateways

A payment gateway is a service that authorizes payments for e-commerce websites. Payment gateways encrypt payment information to ensure that it is transmitted securely over the internet.

Payment gateways are a type of third-party payment system.

Working

  • Visitor places the order.
  • THe customers browser encrypts (usually done via SSL/Secure Socket Layer) the information and send to merchants web server.
  • Merchants server forwards the transactiond details to payment gateway.
  • Payment gateway forwards the transaction information to the payment processor used by the merchants acquiring bank.
  • Merchants acquiring bank then forwards the transaction information to the issuing bank.
  • Issuing bak sends a response (whether the payment has been approved or declined with reason) back to the Payment Gateway.
  • Response is then forwarded by Payment Gateway to merchants server.
  • At merchants server the response is encrypted again and is relayed back to the cusotmer.

Benefits

  • Secure Transactions : Payment gateways encrypt payment information to ensure that it is transmitted securely over the internet.
  • Convenient Payments : Payment gateways allow customers to make payments online using a variety of payment methods like credit cards, debit cards, and e-wallets.
  • Real-Time Processing : Payment gateways process payments in real-time, allowing customers to receive instant confirmation of their transactions.
  • Global Reach : Payment gateways allow businesses to accept payments from customers around the world, increasing their reach and revenue.
  • Integration : Payment gateways can be easily integrated with e-commerce platforms, making it easy for businesses to accept online payments.
  • Cost-Effective : Payment gateways are cost-effective, as they eliminate the need for physical infrastructure like bank branches and reduce the cost of handling cash or cheques.

ATM (Automated Teller Machine) Based Cash disbursed system

It is a system that allows customers to withdraw cash from an ATM using their debit or credit card.

Customers from any bank can use their ATM card to withdraw cash from an ATM.

Working

  • Customer inserts their ATM card into the ATM machine.
  • ATM machine read the magnetic strip from the card and identify customers bank account. Magnetic strips encode customers unique card number along with date of expiry and PIN number.
  • Customer enters their PIN (Personal Identification Number) to authenticate the transaction.
  • Once the PIN is verified, ATM communicates with customers bank to access account information.
  • Customer selects the amount of cash they want to withdraw.
  • ATM forwards the transaction request to host processor which in turn routes the request to cardholders account.

    Host processor is analogous to an ISP (Internet service provider) in that it is the gateway through which all the various ATM networks become available to the cardholder.

  • Host processor then created an electronic funds transfer from cardholders account. After that host processor sends the approval code to ATM to dispense cash.
  • ATM machine dispenses the cash to the customer.
  • ATM machine prints a receipt for the transaction.
  • Customer takes the cash and the receipt and leaves the ATM machine.

Advantages

To Customers

  • Convenience : ATM machines are available 24/7, allowing customers to withdraw cash at any time.
  • Accessibility : ATM machines are located in various locations, making it easy for customers to access their bank accounts.
  • Security : ATM machines use encryption and authentication technologies to ensure the security of transactions.
  • Speed : ATM machines process transactions quickly, allowing customers to withdraw cash in a matter of minutes.
  • Privacy : ATM machines provide customers with privacy when withdrawing cash, as they do not have to interact with bank tellers.
  • Availability : Provides 24x7 to 365 days a year service.

To Bank

  • Cost-Effective : ATM machines reduce the cost of handling cash transactions for banks.
  • Increased Revenue : ATM machines attract customers to the bank, increasing revenue through fees and other services.
  • Customer Retention : ATM machines provide customers with convenient access to their bank accounts, increasing customer retention.
  • Competitive Advantage : ATM machines provide banks with a competitive advantage by offering convenient services to customers.
  • Efficiency : ATM machines process transactions quickly and efficiently, reducing the time and effort required for cash transactions.
  • Crowd : Crowding at back counter considerably reduced.

Disadvantages

  • Security Concerns : ATM machines are vulnerable to theft and fraud, as they are located in public places.
  • Technical Issues : ATM machines can experience technical issues, such as card reader malfunctions or network outages.
  • Transaction Fees : ATM machines may charge transaction fees for cash withdrawals, which can be costly for customers.
  • Accessibility : ATM machines may not be accessible to all customers, especially those with disabilities.
  • Dependence : Customers may become dependent on ATM machines for cash withdrawals, leading to a lack of financial literacy.
  • Spoiled notes : ATM is unable to count spoiled notes.
  • Limited Withdraw : Only limited withdrawal can be transacted on ATM.

Risks involved in E-Payment

A payment risk refer to risk of loss due to some payment event.

The working of the payment systems incurs the following major risks :

Operational Risk

It arises from the potential for unexpected loss due to significant deficiencies in system reliability or integrity.. It may occue due to following reasons :

  • Inadequate system design, maintenance and implementation.
  • Security Risk and Services
  • Operational problems
  • Fraud
  • Customer misuse of Product

Reputation Risk

It arises from the potential for negative public opinion due to the failure of the payment system.

It may occur due to following reasons :

  • Failure to meet customer expectations
  • Failure to meet regulatory requirements
  • Failure to meet contractual obligations
  • Failure to meet legal obligations
  • Failure to meet ethical obligations
  • Failure to meet social obligations
  • Failure to meet environmental obligations
  • Failure to meet financial obligations
  • Failure to meet operational obligations

Legal Risk

It arises from the potential for loss due to legal action against the payment system.

It may occur due to following reasons :

  • Legal rights and obligations are not well established
  • Uncertainty in consumer protection rule for e payment activities
  • Uncertainty in agreements validity
  • Customers having inadequate information about their rights and obligations may bring suit against a bank
  • Failure to provide adequate customer disclosures and privacy protection

Other Risk

  • Credit Risk : It arises from the potential for loss due to the failure of a counterparty to meet its obligations.
  • Liquidity Risk : It arises from the potential for loss due to the inability to meet payment obligations.
  • Market Risk : It arises from the potential for loss due to changes in market conditions.

Issues in E-Payment

User Acceptance

It is the most important issue in e-payment. It is the process of getting users to accept and use e-payment systems.

Security

It is the process of protecting e-payment systems from unauthorized access and fraud.

Security components of e-payments are :

  • Availability
  • Authentication
  • Authorization
  • Encryption and Confidentiality
  • Integrity
  • Non-repudiation

Regulatory and Legal Issue

It is the process of ensuring that e-payment systems comply with laws and regulations.

Some of the major elements include rules on money laundering, supervision of commercial banks and e-money institutions by supervisory authorities, payment system oversight by central banks, consumer and data protection, cooperation and competition issues.
